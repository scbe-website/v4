Common Questions

Frequently Asked

How do I know if I need a panel upgrade? +
Signs you may need an upgrade include frequently tripping breakers, flickering lights, a 100A or smaller panel, plans to add an EV charger or major appliances, or aluminum wiring in an older home. We'll assess your current panel for free and give you a straight answer — no upselling.
Do you pull permits, and why does that matter? +
Yes, always. Permitted work is inspected for code compliance — protecting your safety, your home's insurability, and its resale value. Unpermitted electrical work can void homeowner's insurance and cause problems when selling. We handle all permits and coordinate inspections.
What does an EV charger installation involve? +
We assess your panel capacity, run a dedicated 240V circuit, install the Level 2 charger (hardwired or NEMA 14-50), pull the permit, and coordinate utility approval where required. Most installs are done in a single visit. We work with all brands — Tesla, Ford, Rivian, GM, Hyundai, and more.
Can you add a 30A or 50A RV hookup to my property? +
Yes. We install dedicated 30A (NEMA TT-30) and 50A (NEMA 14-50) RV and trailer electrical hookups at residential and commercial properties throughout Southern California. We run the circuit from your panel, install the correct weather-rated receptacle, and permit everything to NEC code.
Do you work on biotech and laboratory electrical systems? +
Yes — it's one of our specialties. We have 12 years of experience with biotech labs, medical buildings, and research facilities. We understand the requirements for dedicated instrument circuits, isolated power panels, emergency systems, cleanroom-compatible wiring, and coordination with facility engineers and GCs.
What areas do you serve? +
We're based in San Diego and serve all of San Diego County (including East County and North County), Orange County, Los Angeles, San Bernardino, and Riverside. For large commercial projects, reach out regardless of location — we accommodate projects throughout Southern California.
Are you the same as other "Bradley Electric" companies I see online? +
No. SoCal Bradley Electric is an independent, locally owned electrical company based in San Diego, California. We have no affiliation with Bradley Electric companies in other states or regions. Our California license number is #1131979, verifiable at cslb.ca.gov.
